Notable quirks worth flagging

Quirk 01
Huge contact base, no deals

11k+ contacts and zero deal records. Translation: HubSpot is currently more database than CRM. That's a starting position, not a problem — but it does shape what we'd tackle first.

Quirk 02
Almost everything is "Offline Source"

Most recent contacts are tagged offline. That likely means ServiceTitan imports — but it also makes marketing attribution weak.

Worth exploring with Audrey: "Do we know which leads came from Google, the website, calls, referrals, ServiceTitan imports, or past customers?"
Quirk 03
ServiceTitan ID exists, but job context is missing

The CRM knows the customer exists, but doesn't obviously show what service they needed, whether they booked, their last job type, equipment type, or membership status. That's the integration unlock.

Quirk 04
Geography is already there — and useful

Many contacts have city and zip. That alone enables service-area campaigns, zip-density maps, expansion targeting, localized promotions, and territory views.

Quirk 05
HubSpot sample contacts still floating

Maria Johnson, Brian Halligan, etc. Tiny thing, but a cleanup signal worth knocking out early.

Where we could lean in

1 · Separate ecommerce registrations from true sales opportunities — the headline

Pipeline designReportingDeal types
"Right now, deals are doing two jobs: recording purchases and tracking active sales. Separating those would make reporting much cleaner — and let us treat PTC like the real sales pipeline it is."
What's happening today:
  • Low-ticket, instant-purchase records (Online Academy, Bronze, one-day camps) sit next to consultative ~$2,500 PTC deals
  • 518 of 552 deals are closed-won — pipeline reporting will look misleading if used as a sales forecast
What we could explore
  • Separate pipelines or deal types: Registrations / Ecom · Camps · Online Academy · PTC
  • Keep instant purchases out of sales dashboards
  • Program / category fields instead of relying on deal names
What that unlocks
  • Clean revenue-collected vs. active-pipeline split
  • Real PTC forecasting
  • Better roll-ups by program type

Notable quirks worth flagging

518 of 552. Deals are largely registration/payment records. Pipeline reporting will look misleading if used as a sales forecast — separating registrations from sales is the fix.

Quirk 02
Open deals are almost all PTC

The ~34 open deals are dominated by PTC records, often $2,500, often stuck in appointmentscheduled. PTC is where the real sales process lives.

Quirk 03
Deal names are doing too much work

"Competition Hammer Camp - Girls | Kinslee McVey" or "PURLER WRESTLING ACADEMY PROGRAMS - BRONZE | Vaughn Seibel | Hendrix Seibel". Useful for humans, hard for reporting. Program, location, athlete count, add-ons all belong in fields.

Quirk 04
Placeholder emails exist on real customers

May be intentional from the registration flow, but it weakens email marketing and dedupe — worth cleaning up where it matters most.

Quirk 05
Zero tickets

0 tickets on a business that definitely gets parent/camper questions. Could be fine if questions are handled elsewhere — but if Purler wants visibility into common issues, tickets would help.

  • Distinct required fields per pipeline

Notable quirks worth flagging

"Rick Peterson" doesn't tell Katie what kind of opportunity it is. Better: First Last — Service Plan — City.

Quirk 02
Repeated names — repeat business or dupes?

Same homeowner name appears across multiple deals with different amounts. Worth asking Katie which it is.

Quirk 03
Won deals with zero activity logged

Either the team closes outside HubSpot, or imports are skipping the conversation. Hard to diagnose wins/losses without it.

Quirk 04

Kills forecasting and rep-performance reporting. Need required-amount rules at certain stages.

Quirk 05

Add Delay Reason, Revisit Date, Customer Objection, Next Follow-Up Date — or it becomes a junk drawer.

The Chess Read

As a chess player, I naturally look for where the position is strong and where pieces are hanging. The "hanging pieces" in these portals are the deals without next activity dates, missing amounts, expired pilots with no follow-up, and "Delayed" stages with no revisit plan.

"Both portals win the same way — not through one brilliant move, but by avoiding small mistakes repeatedly. Every lead has a stage. Every quote needs a next move. Every delay needs a plan."

Two analogies for Katie

♟
For Noah's Pool Service "Pool service is a sprint with clean fundamentals — every pawn move counts. The opportunity is making follow-up automatic so no homeowner inquiry sits idle while a competitor wins the work."
♚
For Speech Kingdom "Education sales is positional chess. The dangerous squares are Pilot Started, Pilot Expired, Pricing Delivered, and Delayed — places where opportunities quietly disappear without a follow-up plan."

The Portal · Verified Purchase Intelligence

Ourcart

Ourcart helps CPG brands and loyalty programs connect digital marketing to real-world retail purchases. Shoppers submit receipts → Ourcart validates → the platform extracts SKU, retailer, price, basket, location, and timestamp. Brands use that to measure promotions, pay cashback rebates, prevent fraud, and understand shopper behavior. Customers and partners on the site include Catalina, NIQ, P&G, Perfect Snacks, MUSH, Goya, Icelandic Provisions, Barilla, Unilever, Kraft Heinz, OxiClean.

"Most marketing happens online. Most purchases don't."
— Ourcart's positioning. Brands see clicks; they can't prove what sold at retail. Ourcart fills the gap with verified receipts.

Notable quirks worth flagging

Quirk 01
"Nielsen (CONVERT TO MAIN PIPELINE)" is still live

That label is a flashing sign — someone already knows the pipeline is legacy and should be migrated.

Ask Meghan: "Do we know why the Nielsen pipeline still exists, and what criteria should move those deals into Main?"
Quirk 02
Partner-program deals are semi-operational, not purely sales

A sales pipeline asks "Will this close?" A program pipeline asks "Is this campaign live, set up, ended, canceled, paid, and reported?" They can't be managed the same way.

Quirk 03
Seller incentives tracked, but inconsistently

Some Catalina deals have name + email + incentive. Others have name/email but no incentive. Casing and spelling vary. The seller incentive field is supposed to drive Catalina seller payouts — it has to be reliable.

Quirk 04
Many repeated brand/campaign waves

Icelandic Provisions alone has multiple wave/offer records. Goya has many. OxiClean has multiple offer variants. Reporting should roll up at offer, wave, brand, and partner — right now it can't.

Quirk 05
Amounts may be defaults, not real deal economics

Many Catalina program deals are exactly $6,500. That might be a standard package price — or a placeholder hiding real differences in campaign size, incentives, or operational value.

Ask Meghan: "Is $6,500 a standard program amount, a placeholder, or the actual revenue?"
